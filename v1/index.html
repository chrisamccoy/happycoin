Storecoin is a public blockchain for systems governed by hierarchy, privacy, and complex revenue sharing.

These include franchises, chain stores, sports leagues, affiliate-based organizations, and more.

Storecoin is an Ethereum-family technology and will be fully compatible with the Ethereum public network. If you can run a Dapp or smart contract on Ethereum, you can run it on Storecoin.

What We Believe

Franchises, chain stores, sports leagues, and affiliate-based organizations — industries governed by hierarchy, by privacy, by complex revenue sharing, and by commissions — will all eventually be tokenized and powered by blockchain technology.

Storecoin will be the leading public blockchain powering cryptocurrency-based payments, decentralized applications, loyalty points, and market networks for industries representing over 25% of global GDP.

The Problem

Hierarchy-based systems are governed by complex revenue sharing arrangements where commissions and royalties are paid from hub-to-spoke, from spoke-to-hub, and sometimes both -- in the same contract!

Oftentimes, both the amount of the revenue share and the identity of the receiver need to be private.

In larger hierarchies, it’s sometimes a hybrid of both.

Smart Contracts today are public.

They don’t solve for the privacy requirements of hierarchy-based systems like franchises, chain stores, sports leagues, and affiliatebased organizations.

Smart Contracts today are written in code but most of the world doesn’t understand code.

For non-technical teams in hierarchy-based organizations, writing and managing Smart Contracts needs to be straightforward and UX-friendly.

Lawyers have to be able to write and deploy them.

Finally, for hub and spoke-based systems that want to adopt new technology powered by micro-transactions, they have to pay 3% of the total transaction value and an additional $0.30 fee to process each payment.

Economically, any micro-transaction less than $0.30 costs more to process for the organization than the micro-transaction itself.

The initial promise of blockchain technology was free transactions for sender and receiver.

As of July 2017, the lowest per transaction fee using Bitcoin is $2.

It’s $0.14 using Ethereum.

Over time, based upon their technology and economics, these fees will only grow.

Our Solution

Storecoin solves this by enabling transactions in a smart contract to be private, public or both.

Also, by enabling unlimited levels of revenue sharing in unlimited directions.

All using fully customizable, UI friendly smart contracts.

Storecoin does this with zero transaction fees to the sender and to the receiver.

Our Technology

To scale our blockchain, Storecoin is committed to minimizing on-chain data. Instead our focus on scaling is through off-chain transactions by embracing emergent blockchain technologies such as State Channels.

Click on the icons below to learn more about our technology.

Version 1 of Storecoin will use Tendermint, a new consensus protocol for ordering events in a distributed network under adversarial conditions.

Tendermint is high performance, achieving thousands of transactions per second on dozens of nodes distributed around the globe, with latencies of about one second, and performance degrading moderately in the face of adversarial attacks.

Storecoin may shift to a Proof of Stake consensus in future versions.

For privacy, we’re experimenting with new cryptography inside smart contracts. Transactions on the public network, outside of smart contracts, will always be public.

To achieve unlimited nodes of hierarchy in smart contracts, we’re using a new database design for smart contracts and a new UX for their creation and deployment.

To achieve free transactions for senders and receivers, each transaction will generate some inflation which will be distributed to the participants of the consensus. The total new token inflation will be lower than 5% each year and it will be directly linked to blockchain transactions.

For loyalty points, we’re designing a ERC20-like platform for consumer packaged goods to be tied to provenance. If the token changes wallets, only the active key can be used in Dapps.
